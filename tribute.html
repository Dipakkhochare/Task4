Ada Lovelace

Mathematician, Writer, and the World's First Computer Programmer

Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace (10 December 1815 â€“ 27 November 1852) was an English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

Her notes on the engine include what is now considered to be the first algorithm intended to be processed by a machine. Because of this, she is often regarded as the world's first computer programmer.

Ada Lovelace passed away at the age of 36 but her contributions to computer science continue to inspire generations.
